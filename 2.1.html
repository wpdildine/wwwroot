Follow the Lead

The Steps to Successful Floortime

Floortime is play, but it is play with a purpose. With this training course, you learn to tune into your child’s emotions and interests. By responding to these basic motivations, you open a new world for you and your child. Begin by following your child’s lead.

Following your child’s lead might sound pretty easy.

Following your child’s lead might sound pretty easy. In an ideal world, your child might decide what to do and you play along. He’s the cop, you’re the robber. She’s the captain of the ship, you’re the crew. When you’re following the lead of an autistic child, though, things may not be so simple. They may have a hard time getting started; they may want to do the same thing over and over.

So how do you follow the lead? What lead?

General Techniques

Play Audio Clip Following the child’s lead Play Audio Clip Physically directing the child
  • Identify what the child is doing.
  • Make sure the child is calm; if not, soothe first
  • Join the child’s interest and activity (sensory, objects, or words/ideas).
    Don’t just watch and comment.
  • Treat the child’s every action (even repetitive motions) as purposeful.
  • Use your voice and facial expressions to become the most interesting object in the room.
  • Respond to your child by raising or lowering your energy level and voice
  • Use language and gestures (movements) together
  • Encourage your child, but don’t control his play
