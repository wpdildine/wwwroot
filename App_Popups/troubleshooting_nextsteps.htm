You have taken your first steps in providing Floortime therapy for your child. This is an evolving process. There are always more opportunities to help your child develop and grow. Here are other steps you can take to hone your skills and knowledge.

  1. 1Practice Floortime every day or as often as you can.
  2. 2Review the video examples you have already watched. Having done your own Floortime, you may see subtleties in the interaction that you missed the first time.
  3. 3Explore the video examples in the other types of play. Even if your child is capable of symbolic play, he or she may also enjoy sensory and/or object-based play. If you’re not sure your child is ready for symbolic play, make sure you listen to Dr. Greenspan’s advice on the child’s readiness for this type of play; if your child is not yet ready, pushing too hard can slow or even halt progress.
  4. 4Explore solutions for other attentional and behavioral problems.
  5. 5Go to Resources for additional information on Floortime and the DIR model, such as the Learning Tree video and video-based classes for parents and professionals.
  6. 6Check out these books. Dr. Greenspan’s books offer reinforcement and more details for the Floortime principles highlighted in the videos.
  7. 7Contact ‘The Floortime Center’ for clinical help.
